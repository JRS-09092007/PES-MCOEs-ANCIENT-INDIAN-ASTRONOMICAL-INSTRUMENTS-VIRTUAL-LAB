The Digamsa Yantra

Built in the early 18th century by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, this instrument calculates the Azimuth (horizontal angle) of the sun and stars.

The astronomer walks in the space between the inner and outer walls, aligning a thread from the central cross-wires to the celestial body, reading the angle on the graduated circular walls.

Astronomical Principles

The simulation uses standard solar position equations:

  • Calculates Day of Year (N)
  • Derives Solar Declination ($\delta$)
  • Calculates Local Hour Angle (H) based on Longitude and Time
  • Derives Solar Altitude ($\alpha$) and Azimuth (A) using spherical trigonometry based on observer Latitude.

Structural Accuracy

  • Central Pillar: Holds the center node.
  • Inner Wall: Matching height to pillar.
  • Outer Wall: Taller, anchors the North-South and East-West crosswires.
  • Entrances: 4 cardinal cutouts allowing the observer to enter.
  • Virtual Observer: The blue figure aligns the measurement thread.
