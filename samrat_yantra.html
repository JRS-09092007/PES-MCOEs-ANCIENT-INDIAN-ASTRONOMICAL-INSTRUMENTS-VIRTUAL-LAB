Architectural Design

The Samrat Yantra is built on the precise principles of an equatorial sundial, essentially acting as a physical representation of the Earth's coordinate system.

Structural Geometry

The Gnomon (Central Triangle): The sloping hypotenuse edge points exactly to the North Celestial Pole. The angle of this slope relative to the ground is always equal to the observatory's local latitude.

The Quadrants: The two curved scales form a plane perfectly perpendicular to the gnomon, making them parallel to the Earth's equator. This ensures the sun's shadow moves at a uniform rate of 15 degrees per hour.

The Need for Massive Scale

The Jaipur Samrat Yantra is 27 meters (89 ft) tall. Due to this massive radius, the shadow sweeps across the quadrant at approximately 1 millimeter per second.

This macro-scale physical design allowed astronomers to visually see time passing and record local solar time to an astonishing precision of just 2 seconds.
