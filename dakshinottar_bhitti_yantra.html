The Math behind the Yantra:

The instrument measures the Zenith Distance (z) strictly when the Sun crosses the Local Meridian (Hour Angle = 0, at exactly Solar Noon).

  • Observer Latitude = φ
  • Solar Declination = δ
  • Zenith Distance = 90° - Altitude

The Dakshinottar Bhitti Yantra is a meridian transit instrument constructed in ancient Indian observatories (Jantar Mantars) by Maharaja Jai Singh II in the early 18th century.

Architectural Design

  • Built exactly on the North-South meridian line.
  • West Face: Features a semicircular scale. Used for general measurements.
  • East Face: Features intersecting quadrants with two gnomons (Northern and Southern). Designed to track the sun regardless of whether it is in the northern or southern hemisphere.
