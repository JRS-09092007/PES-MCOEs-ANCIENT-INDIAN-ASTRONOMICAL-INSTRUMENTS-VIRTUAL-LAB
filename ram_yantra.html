Real Ram Yantra Architecture

To use the Ram Yantra, an astronomer must physically walk inside the structure to read the shadow marks. If it were a solid bowl, this would be impossible without blocking the light.

  • The Ingenious Solution: The walls and floors are cut into 12 sectors of 30° each. Every alternate sector is left completely empty (like a gap in a fence) so a person can walk through.
  • The Complementary Pair: Because half the walls are missing, a second identical instrument (Instrument B) is built next to it. Where Instrument A has a solid wall, Instrument B has a gap, and vice versa.
  • How this simulator works: Both structures are rendered side-by-side. As the sun sweeps across the sky, watch how one ray hits a wall/floor while the identical ray in the other building falls straight through the gap. The camera automatically follows the valid reading!
