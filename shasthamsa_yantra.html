What is the Shasthamsa Yantra?

The Shasthamsa Yantra is a traditional Indian astronomical instrument (Yantra) designed to measure the zenith distance and altitude of the Sun with extreme precision.

"Shasthamsa" literally translates to "sixtieth part", referring to a 60-degree arc, making it conceptually similar to a classical Sextant.

How it Works:

In its grand architectural form (like at Jaipur), it consists of a 60-degree graduated arc built in the meridian plane inside a dark chamber. Sunlight enters through a pinhole, casting a precise spot on the finely divided arc.

This 3D simulator models the geometric principle: an arc divided into very fine units (1/6th of a degree) with an alignment arm (representing the sightline or light beam) pointing directly at the celestial object.

Measurements:

  • Altitude (α): Angle of the Sun above the horizon.
  • Zenith Distance (z): Angle from the directly overhead point (zenith). z = 90° - α
  • Precision: The scale is divided such that 1 unit = 10 arcminutes (1/6°).
