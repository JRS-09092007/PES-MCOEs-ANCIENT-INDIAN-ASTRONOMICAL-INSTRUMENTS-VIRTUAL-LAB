The Hollow Hemispheres

The Jai Prakash Yantra represents the pinnacle of Indian observational astronomy, functioning as a complete celestial sphere mapped onto the earth.

Why Hollow Bowls? The instrument consists of two sunken, hollow hemispheres. If it were a single continuous bowl, an astronomer couldn't step inside to precisely read the shadow without blocking the light or slipping on the curved marble.

The Genius Solution: Jai Singh carved deep walkways into the surface, removing alternating 15-degree wedges. To ensure no data was lost in these gaps, he built a second identical hollow bowl (Bowl B) where the solid wedges and walkways are perfectly inverted. An astronomer steps down into the dark sunken walkways of one bowl to read the shadow falling on the adjacent solid white marble sectors.
